Yosenabe

What is Yosenabe?

Yosenabe is a Japanese word hot pot, or more accurately yose means gather, nabe means hot pot. The word itself is self-explanatory since it is is simply a collection of ingredients in a single pot. It is a timeless traditonal Japanese dish!

Ingredients for Yosenabe

These are some suggestions for your yosenabe, but it can be replaced with anything you like.

  • Any seafood: cod, snapper, prawns, clamps, mussels or squid.
  • Any meat: chicken, pork or beef.
  • Any tofu: firm tofu, soft tofu or aburaage.
  • Any vegetables: cabbages, carrots or enoki mushrooms.

Cooking Yosenabe

There are two general methods of cooking yosenabe, pick whichever suits your style!

Method 1

  1. Place all ingredients in the pot.
  2. Add soup.
  3. Cook all ingredients in one go.

Method 2

  1. Add soup to the pot.
  2. Heat the soup up.
  3. Place the ingredients on a plate.
  4. Let the diners cook it themselves on the dining table!

And there you have it, your first yosenabe!
